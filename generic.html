About Veltrix

Veltrix was founded in San Francisco in 2014 by a group of engineers who had grown frustrated with the gap between how enterprise software projects were sold and how they were actually delivered. Too often, clients signed contracts with large consultancies, received a team of junior developers managed from thousands of miles away, and ended up with systems that worked in demos but buckled under production load. We built Veltrix to be the firm we wished had existed when we were on the client side.

Today, our team of 47 engineers and architects works with a deliberately limited number of clients at any given time. We believe depth of engagement produces better outcomes than breadth of portfolio. Every project has a named technical lead who is accountable for delivery and who is accessible directly — not through a project management layer. Our typical engagement runs six to eighteen months, and roughly 70% of our revenue comes from clients we've worked with before.

Our engineering practice is organized around three disciplines: cloud infrastructure and platform engineering, data systems, and product software. We don't do digital transformation strategy, organizational change management, or branding. We write code and ship systems. If you need someone to help you figure out what to build before we build it, we can do a scoped discovery engagement — but our core value is execution, not facilitation.
